Warren Buffett's Guide to Early Investing

1. The "Long Hill" of Compound Interest

Buffett bought his first stock at age 11, yet jokes he wasted his life until then. He compares wealth to rolling a snowball: "The important thing is finding wet snow and a really long hill." Time is that long hill.

2. Generate Capital Through Hustle

Before he was a billionaire, he delivered papers and placed pinball machines in barbershops. You don't need to be rich to start—you just need creative ways to generate initial seed capital.

3. Reinvest Your Profits

When his pinball machine made a profit, he didn't spend it. He immediately bought a second machine. Build a habit of delayed gratification and reinvest your returns.

4. Patience Over Quick Wins

His first stock dropped, he held it until it rebounded, sold for a small profit, and then watched it soar much higher later. His empire was built on buying fundamentally good companies and holding them for decades.
